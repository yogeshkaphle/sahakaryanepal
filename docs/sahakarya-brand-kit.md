- **Established:** 2061/06/24 BS (10 Oct 2004 AD). ~20 years.
- **Type:** Non-governmental, non-profit development organization

**The brand's #1 job:** institutional credibility for donor due-diligence. Community trust second. The real differentiator to lean on: a two-decade, female-led track record serving the most marginalized. Show the record, not adjectives.

## 2. The logo

**Do NOT redesign the emblem as part of this website build.** It's a 20-year institutional asset on registration certificates, signboards, letterhead, and audited donor reports under Reg. No. 363. Recognition is trust for an org this established. A logo refresh is a board decision with real cost, scoped separately, never a build side-effect. Yes, the emblem is amateur clip-art (the UN-style globe + laurel wreath is generic to Nepali NGOs), but the fix is a governed rebrand, not a quiet swap.

**The symbols:** globe (global outlook) · Nepal map in green (the land) · Himalaya + rising sun (aspiration) · **two cupped orange hands holding Nepal** (care, cooperation — the truest and most distinctive element) · blue wreath (honor) · wordmark.

### Website approach (the defensible minor moves)
1. **The site palette does not copy the logo's raw colors.** Standard practice: a refined digital system harmonious with the logo but built for screen, accessibility, and donor tone (§3). The full-color emblem sits inside a white container on the site; the refined palette lives everywhere else.
2. **A simplified digital mark** for favicon / social avatar / app icon (the detailed emblem dies below ~48px). Derive it from the **cupped hands** element, the one piece with real equity. The official emblem stays official; this is its small-size sibling.

### Usage rules for the emblem
- Clear space equal to the wordmark height on all sides.
- Place on white / very light grounds only; on color or photo, use a white container or solid scrim.
- Never recolor, stretch, skew, add effects, re-typeset the wordmark, or lift a fragment (until the simplified mark exists).

## 3. Color palette (refined for web — measured against WCAG AA)

| Role | Hex | Contrast on white | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Deep Blue** (primary) | `#16386E` | 11.5:1 | the institutional anchor — headers, primary buttons, links, structure, trust. |
| **Hill Green** (secondary) | `#2F6B45` | 6.3:1 | the work and the land — programs, impact, agriculture/livelihood/climate content, success states. |
| **Marigold** (accent) | `#E4832A` | 2.8:1 | the human warmth — people, stories, get-involved. Accent only. Needs dark text on top; not for small text or white text. |
| **Deep Marigold** (CTA variant) | `#B85F17` | 4.5:1 | marigold buttons that carry white label text (AA for large/bold). |
| **Ink** (text) | `#1D2430` | 15.6:1 | body and headings. Warm near-black, not pure black. |
| **Slate** (muted) | `#55606E` | 6.4:1 | captions, metadata, secondary text. |
| **Stone** (light bg) | `#F5F3EE` | — | subtle section backgrounds, cards. Used sparingly, not a full cream aesthetic. |
| **White** | `#FFFFFF` | — | primary background, logo container. |

**Rules:**
- **One color, one job.** Blue leads every screen. Green marks the work. Marigold marks the one people-focused action. If everything is marigold, nothing is.
- **One accent per view.** A screen has one marigold moment, not five.
- **Accessibility is mandatory** (donor/government scrutiny, older and low-bandwidth users). Every text/background pair meets AA. Marigold never carries small text; pair it with Ink on top, or use Deep Marigold for white-on-color labels.
- The electric logo cobalt `#062AC4` is retired for UI. It survives only inside the emblem itself.

## 4. Typography

**Primary: Noto Sans + Noto Sans Devanagari.** [Likely — best fit]
- Free, full Devanagari coverage (Nepali is first-class here, not an afterthought), highly legible, institutionally trustworthy, and it avoids the AI-default NGO trio (Poppins / Montserrat / Nunito).
- Body: Noto Sans 400/500, line-height 1.6 for mobile. Headings: Noto Sans 700, tight tracking. Nepali: Noto Sans Devanagari, matched weights.

**Alternative (more gravitas):** serif English headings (Source Serif 4 or Lora) + Noto Serif Devanagari for Nepali headings + Noto Sans body. More "established institution," more complexity to keep from looking generic. Pick one, lock it.

*Boldness is spent on the palette meaning and the hands motif, not on risky type. Type stays disciplined and legible.*

## 5. Design direction (avoid the AI-default NGO look)

Subject: a female-led, 20-year cooperative development org in the hills of Sindhuli, serving the most marginalized, funded by serious international donors. Ground every choice there.

- **Not** the generic nonprofit hero (blue-gradient + stock smiling-children photo + "empowering communities for a brighter tomorrow"). Banned.
- **Not** the AI clusters: near-black + acid accent; cream + serif + terracotta; broadsheet hairlines.
- **Do** lead with the real record: real photographs of real work in Sindhuli, real numbers (300,000 people, 50 projects, 20 years), named donors as trust proof, transparency (audited financials, registration numbers). For a donor-facing NGO, specificity *is* the aesthetic.
- **Signature element candidate:** the **cupped hands** motif, abstracted into a structural device that holds/cradles content, stats, and section headers. Decide in the design-system step. One memorable element, everything else quiet.
- **Female leadership is real texture**, not a banner. Let it show through real photos and named people, not a slogan.
- **Mobile-first, low-bandwidth-first.** Nepal is mobile-majority on slow connections. Fast static Astro output, compressed images, works on a cheap Android over 3G.
- **Accessibility:** WCAG AA, visible keyboard focus, reduced-motion respected.
